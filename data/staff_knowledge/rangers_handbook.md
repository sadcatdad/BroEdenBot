# Ranger's Handbook

> Staff-only operational guidance for Rangers and Scouts in training.
> Source: `📔・rangers-handbook` in Headquarters, exported May 29, 2026.
> This is internal guidance, not public member-facing policy. Staff should
> confirm uncertain or high-impact decisions with the Ranger/Admin team.

## Community leadership

Rangers and Scouts are community leaders, not only technical moderators. New
members may form their impression of the staff team and server through these
roles.

- Lead with kindness.
- Be welcoming and helpful.
- Do not abuse moderation powers.
- Do not power-flex publicly or let ego drive staff actions.
- Help foster an inclusive community.
- Ask other Rangers or Admins when uncertain.

## Verification

When receiving a verification ticket from an Unverified member:

- Check that both required photos match the current verification example.
- Confirm that all three application-form questions are answered.
- If the applicant says he is joining only for NSFW content, explain the NSFW
  policy and confirm that he still wants to join the broader community. Use
  staff judgment and consult another Ranger when needed.
- Once the photo and application requirements are satisfied, the member may be
  verified.
- If a ticket was just opened, staff may ping the applicant and tell him he can
  begin.
- The usual submission window is 24 hours. Reasonable reminders or extra time
  may be given.
- If the applicant omitted the username, date, server name, or another required
  element, request corrected photos.
- A passport may be accepted when it clearly shows date of birth, face, and
  country.
- A school ID may occasionally be accepted with careful judgment when it shows
  at least the applicant's face and date of birth and appears legitimate.
- IDs showing required information on separate sides should be photographed
  together beside the same verification paper.
- Verification must not be completed by video or private DM. Staff need a
  reviewable record that can be cross-checked.
- If Discord cannot safely display the submitted file type, ask the applicant
  to resend it as a compatible image such as JPEG, PNG, or GIF instead of
  downloading it.

### Image authenticity and safety

- Look carefully for AI editing, Photoshop, or other manipulation.
- Warning signs may include hard cut lines, missing shadows, inconsistent
  textures, or unnatural paper/ID edges.
- Get multiple staff eyes on suspicious or uncertain submissions.
- Do not approve a submission merely because the applicant is impatient.

### Foreign IDs

If a foreign ID uses an unfamiliar date-of-birth format, use the country shown
on the ID to find a trustworthy reference for that country's ID layout. Ask
another Ranger when the document remains unclear.

### Verification commands

- `?v @user` — Verify the member by removing Unverified and adding Verified.
  The username mention is required.
- If the verification command is unavailable, staff may manually remove
  Unverified and add Verified.
- `!v` — Post the visual verification reminder in unverified chat.
- `!submit @user` — Remind an applicant to submit verification photos.
- `!example @user` — Ask an applicant to resubmit photos following the example.
- `!nsfw @user` — Respond when an application suggests the applicant is joining
  only for NSFW access.

## Welcoming verified members

In the arrivals channel:

1. Ping the newly verified member with a welcoming message.
2. Ping the Welcome Hug role.
3. Include the words `check out` so the menu bot posts its navigation prompt.

## Unverified chat

Unverified chat is the only conversational channel visible to many new
applicants and creates their first impression of the server.

- Keep it reasonably active when possible.
- Ping Unverified members with friendly verification reminders.
- Explain that the full server becomes available after verification.
- Have light conversation with newcomers about interests, games, and what
  brought them to the community.
- Remind applicants that sensitive ID information may be covered as long as the
  required verification information remains visible.
- Using the word `ticket` summons the ticket guidance.
- Use `!v` to keep the visual verification reminder visible.
- Review blocked AutoMod activity in mod-log when it suggests a genuinely
  concerning applicant, then use staff judgment.

## Support tickets

The handbook identifies these support-ticket categories:

- Server inquiries and troubleshooting
- Report a user
- Voice concerns to staff
- Other

### Creating a ticket and adding a member

When staff need to address a member privately:

- Create a support ticket.
- Use `/add` to add the member.
- Explain the concern or behavior being addressed.
- Stay respectful and avoid power-flexing.

### Closing tickets

When a support ticket is resolved:

- Tell the member the ticket will be closed.
- Ask whether he has final questions or comments.
- Ping him so he receives the notice.
- Close and transcript the ticket through the normal workflow.

### Escalating to Admins

Create an Admin Support ticket when:

- A higher-level concern should not be discussed in the Ranger lounge.
- A staff member is uncomfortable discussing the issue there.
- An existing ticket needs Admin attention; use `/escalate`.
- A serious concern involving a verified member cannot appropriately be handled
  in a normal support ticket.

Admins are available to help, and Rangers should escalate when needed.

## Rest Pass and temporary access removal

For a potentially volatile member who may have a public outburst after being
confronted, staff may prepare a Rest Pass:

- Remove Verified.
- Remove NSFW access.
- Remove level roles such as Adventurer or Luminary.
- Add Rest Pass.

This restricts normal server access while preserving access to the designated
Peace Meadow area. Use this as a deliberate staff action, not an automatic
response.

## SFW moderation

- Keep SFW discussion channels clear of blatant sexual conversation.
- A light public reminder may redirect members to an appropriate NSFW channel.
- SFW media channels should not contain explicit or strongly sexual imagery,
  including exposed genitals, pubic hair, prominent sexual bulges, or clearly
  suggestive content.
- Staff may remove inappropriate SFW media and open a support ticket to notify
  the member.
- Ask in a staff lounge when an image is borderline.
- New members immediately seeking sexual DMs or sexual VC interaction may need
  a private reminder that Bro Eden is a community rather than a hookup server.
- Casual, non-sexual shirtlessness in voice chat is allowed. Sexualized
  performance is not.

## NSFW moderation

- The main NSFW lounge may allow non-pornographic nudity such as memes or art
  photography.
- Member self-nudes or sexual teases do not belong in the general NSFW lounge;
  redirect them to the appropriate Neon District channel.
- Other NSFW media may be redirected to the designated darkroom channel when
  appropriate.

## Redirecting media comments

If a member comments in a media-only channel:

- Ping or redirect him to the appropriate comment channel.
- Give a gentle reminder.
- Remove the misplaced original comment when appropriate.

## Voice chat capacity

When a voice channel has reached its member cap, staff—including Admins—should
not join merely because they are staff. Staff may enter a full channel when a
situation requires moderation, mediation, or intervention.

## Hiatus role

When a member requests Hiatus:

- Add the Hiatus role.
- Remove Verified.
- Remove NSFW access.
- Remove level roles such as Luminary.

## Members who leave and rejoin

- Returning members must complete verification again, even when staff recognize
  them.
- Their level progression restarts from zero.
- Remove prior levels through the configured level-bot workflow.
- Remove prior NSFW access.

## Kicking and banning

- Verified members should normally be kicked or banned after Ranger-team
  agreement.
- Use Discord Mod View or the configured `/ban` command.
- When using `/ban`, select the member or provide the user ID and include a
  reason so the action is documented and the member can see why.
- A problematic Unverified applicant may be removed without a full team
  consultation when immediate behavior clearly warrants it, such as gross
  sexual solicitation or serious AutoMod-blocked conduct.

## Staff discussions and decisions

Bring server concerns or problematic member behavior to the Ranger discussion
channels. The goal is to reach consequential decisions as a team rather than
acting alone.

## Staff breaks and retirement

- Rangers may request a break from tickets or moderation duties. Communicate
  the break so the team knows what coverage to expect.
- A retiring Ranger may receive the optional Legacy Ranger role after at least
  one active month.
- Legacy Rangers retain access to Ranger discussion and may participate in
  decisions and votes, but lose moderation powers and ticket duties.

- `!5twilight` / `!5midnight` — Address apparent level-five speed-running in
  the corresponding lounge.

- `!upvote` — Send an upvote reminder to the Bump Bros role.
- `!send [text]` — Send a message through the bot. Use carefully and only for
